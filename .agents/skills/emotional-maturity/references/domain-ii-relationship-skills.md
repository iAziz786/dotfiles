# Domain II: Relationship Skills (Traits 6-12)

Detailed examples for relationship skills.

## 6. Secure Attachment Behaviors

**Actively seeks partner's presence during stress (proximity-seeking)**
- EM has a terrible day at work, calls their partner and says "I need you right now," goes to their partner for comfort rather than isolating
- EM faces a medical scare and wants their partner in the room with them, not because they can't handle it alone, but because sharing it makes it more bearable
- EM experiences a panic attack and texts their partner "Can you come over? I need your presence" rather than hiding it

**Uses partner as secure base to explore the world (safe haven)**
- EM takes a solo travel adventure, knowing their partner is their home base to return to and share stories with
- EM starts a new business venture, feels supported by their partner's belief in them, which gives courage to take risks
- EM goes to a social event alone, knowing they can check in with their partner later for emotional processing and validation

**Both seeks support AND provides support to distressed partner (reciprocity)**
- EM receives comfort from partner after their own hard day, then later that evening notices partner seems withdrawn and asks "Are you okay? I'm here for you"
- EM is grieving and needs support, but when their partner gets laid off, EM shifts into support mode despite their own pain
- EM and partner take turns being the "strong one" during a family crisis, recognizing that neither can be strong all the time

## 7. Interdependence vs. Codependence

**Practices dyadic coping — joint stress management where both contribute**
- EM and partner face financial stress, sit down together to create a budget, both contribute ideas, both make sacrifices
- EM gets diagnosed with an illness, partner attends doctor appointments, researches treatments together, shares emotional burden
- EM and partner both have demanding jobs, create a shared system for managing household tasks that plays to both strengths

**Supports partner's autonomy and independent goals**
- EM's partner wants to take a month-long solo retreat, EM feels some anxiety about the separation but genuinely supports their growth
- EM encourages partner to maintain close friendships outside the relationship, doesn't feel threatened by partner's independent social life
- EM celebrates partner's promotion even though it means less time together, doesn't sabotage or resent the success

**Maintains clear boundaries and can say "no" without fear of abandonment**
- EM's partner wants to move in together after 3 months, EM says "I love you and I'm not ready for that yet. I need more time," and trusts the relationship can handle the boundary
- EM declines to attend their partner's work event because they have their own commitment, doesn't feel guilty or make excuses
- EM tells partner "I need an hour alone after work to decompress before we talk about our days," and partner respects this without taking it personally

## 8. Capacity for Intimacy and Vulnerability

**Willing to share fears, needs, and insecurities (emotional disclosure)**
- EM tells partner "I'm afraid you'll realize I'm not as successful as you thought and leave me"
- EM shares with close friend "I felt really hurt when you forgot my birthday, and I'm telling you because our friendship matters more than my pride"
- EM tells therapist "I'm terrified of being ordinary, of living a life that doesn't matter"

**Allows self to be seen fully despite fear of rejection (risk-taking)**
- EM shows their partner their unfinished creative work, risking judgment, because they want to be known fully
- EM tells a date about their mental health history on the third date, not to scare them away, but because hiding it feels like lying
- EM shares a childhood trauma with their partner, shaking, knowing it might change how they're seen, but values authenticity over protection

**Responds to partner's vulnerability with attunement, not judgment or withdrawal**
- Partner cries and says "I feel like I'm failing at everything," EM sits close, listens fully, says "I see how hard you're trying. You're not alone in this"
- Partner admits "I'm jealous of your success," EM doesn't get defensive or shame them, says "Thank you for trusting me with that. Let's talk about what's underneath it"
- Partner shares a shameful secret, EM responds with "I'm honored you told me. That doesn't change how I see you. How can I support you?"

## 9. Maintaining Separate Identities While Connected

**Intrapsychic differentiation: maintains clear sense of self during emotional reactivity**
- EM and partner are fighting, partner says something hurtful, EM feels the sting but doesn't lose themselves—they think "That's their pain talking, not the truth about me"
- EM is at a family gathering where old dynamics activate, feels the pull to revert to childhood role, but maintains adult identity
- EM partner expresses a strong opinion that contradicts EM's values, EM doesn't automatically agree or rebel, but stays connected to their own position

**Maintains independent friendships, hobbies, and personal goals**
- EM has a weekly dinner with college friends that partner doesn't attend, maintains these relationships as separate from the couple
- EM pursues a solo hobby (painting, running, gaming) that partner doesn't share, doesn't require partner's participation or approval
- EM applies for a promotion that might require relocation, discusses with partner but ultimately makes decision based on their own career goals

**Can self-soothe without requiring partner to change**
- EM feels anxious, practices their own breathing exercises and grounding techniques rather than demanding partner reassure them repeatedly
- EM is triggered by partner's behavior, recognizes it's their own wound, works with it internally or with therapist before addressing with partner
- EM has a bad day, goes for a walk and journals, returns to partner regulated rather than dumping emotional chaos on them

## 10. Mutual Support Without Caretaking

**Accessible: physically and emotionally available (not withdrawn)**
- EM puts down their phone when partner wants to talk, makes eye contact, is fully present
- EM doesn't stonewall during conflict—stays in the conversation even when uncomfortable
- EM checks in daily with "How are you really doing?" creating consistent availability

**Responsive: attunes to partner's emotional signals**
- EM notices partner's voice sounds flat, asks "You seem off. What's going on?" rather than ignoring it
- EM sees partner struggling with a task, asks "Do you want help or space?" rather than either doing it for them or ignoring the struggle
- EM remembers partner mentioned a work stress a week ago, follows up "How did that presentation go?"

**Engaged: actively participates without overfunctioning**
- EM helps partner with a project, asks "What do you need from me?" rather than taking over entirely
- EM supports partner through grief, listens and validates, doesn't try to "fix" their feelings or make them stop crying
- EM and partner cook dinner together, both contribute, neither dominates or rescues

## 11. Navigating Closeness and Distance

**Recognizes pursuer-distancer cycle when it activates**
- EM notices they're pursuing partner for affection, partner is withdrawing, and says "I think we're in our old pattern. Can we slow this down and try again differently?"
- EM is the distancer this time, recognizes it, and chooses to move toward instead: "I'm feeling the urge to pull away. I'm going to stay and talk instead"
- EM identifies the cycle in real-time: "You're pushing for answers, I'm shutting down. Let's both take a breath and find the middle"

**Softens pursuit: expresses needs without criticism, blame, or demand**
- Instead of "You never make time for me!" EM says "I miss connecting with you. Can we plan something together this weekend?"
- Rather than "Why don't you ever initiate?" EM says "I'd love it if sometimes you planned our dates. It would make me feel desired"
- Instead of demanding attention during partner's work crisis, EM says "I need some connection when you have space. I'll be here when you're ready"

**Tolerates both intimacy and separateness**
- EM enjoys deep conversations and physical closeness with partner, and also values time alone without anxiety
- EM is comfortable with partner going on a trip with friends, doesn't need constant contact, trusts the bond
- EM can be in different rooms of the house for hours, working on separate projects, feeling connected without proximity

## 12. Long-Term Relationship Maintenance

**Maintains detailed knowledge of partner's world, hopes, dreams (love maps)**
- EM knows partner's current work stressors, their childhood friend's name, what they want to achieve in the next 5 years
- EM asks partner "Whatever happened with that project you were nervous about last month?"
- EM remembers partner's favorite childhood comfort food and makes it when they're sick

**Expresses appreciation and respect regularly (fondness and admiration)**
- EM tells partner "I love how you handled that difficult conversation with such grace"
- EM texts partner midday: "Thinking about how lucky I am to have you"
- EM says to friends in front of partner: "I'm so proud of how dedicated they are to their work"

**Creates shared rituals, roles, and goals (shared meaning)**
- EM and partner have a Sunday morning ritual: coffee, crossword puzzle, no phones
- EM and partner define their roles intentionally: "I'll handle finances, you handle social calendar, we'll decide major purchases together"
- EM and partner create a shared 5-year vision board, including individual and joint goals
