# Domain VII: Boundaries and Assertiveness (Traits 45-51)

Detailed examples for boundary setting and assertive communication.

## 45. Healthy Boundary Setting & Maintenance

**Clear, flexible boundaries that permit functioning within subsystems**
- EM has clear work hours and doesn't check email during family dinner, but is accessible during true emergencies
- EM shares personal information with close friends but not acquaintances, with flexibility based on trust level
- EM participates in family obligations while maintaining separate adult identity and choices

**Well-defined boundaries that encapsulate problems without proliferation**
- EM and partner have conflict about finances, they keep it between them, don't involve children or vent to friends in ways that undermine partner
- EM has issue with coworker, addresses it directly with them, doesn't gossip or triangulate with other colleagues
- EM's family has drama, EM stays informed but doesn't take on responsibility for solving it all

**Can maintain boundaries under pressure**
- EM's parent pressures them to visit more, EM holds boundary: "I love you and I'm visiting monthly. That's what works for me"
- EM's boss asks them to work weekend, EM declines even though it might affect their review: "I have commitments I can't break"
- EM's friends push them to drink, EM says "I'm not drinking tonight" and doesn't cave to peer pressure

## 46. Assertive Communication

**Direct, respectful communication balancing self-respect with relationship maintenance**
- EM tells friend "I felt hurt when you canceled last minute. I value our time together and I need advance notice if plans change"
- EM asks boss "Can we discuss my workload? I'm concerned about meeting deadlines with current assignments"
- EM tells partner "I need physical affection. Can we make time for that this week?"

**Uses DEAR MAN framework**
- **D**escribe: "You interrupted me three times in the meeting"
- **E**xpress: "I felt dismissed and frustrated"
- **A**ssert: "I need you to let me finish my thoughts"
- **R**einforce: "When you listen fully, we have better collaboration"
- **M**indful: stays on topic, doesn't bring up past grievances
- **A**ppear confident: maintains eye contact, steady voice
- **N**egotiate: "If timing is issue, we can discuss offline"

**Interpersonal effectiveness without aggression or passivity**
- EM states needs clearly without apologizing excessively (passive) or demanding aggressively
- EM can say "I want..." and "I don't want..." with equal comfort
- EM expresses disagreement without attacking character: "I see it differently" not "You're wrong and stupid"

## 47. Ability to Say No Without Guilt

**Recognizes guilt as signal someone wants something you're not comfortable giving**
- EM is asked to loan money they can't afford, feels guilt, recognizes it as manipulation, says "I can't do that"
- EM is pressured to attend event they don't want to go to, feels guilt, recognizes it as others' disappointment, not their obligation, says "I won't be there"
- EM's family wants them to change career, EM feels guilt, recognizes it's family's unmet need, not their responsibility, says "I appreciate your concern and I'm following my path"

**Has specific scripts/phrases for declining**
- "I can't take that on right now"
- "That doesn't work for me"
- "I need to decline"
- "I'm not available"
- "I'd rather not"

**Can refuse without excessive guilt or explanation**
- EM says "No" without 5-minute apology or detailed excuse
- EM says "I can't" and stops, doesn't over-explain or justify
- EM declines and doesn't ruminate afterward, doesn't need to be "the good person" to everyone

## 48. Accepting Others' Boundaries

**Does not personalize others' boundary-setting**
- Friend says "I can't talk tonight," EM thinks "They have their own life" not "They don't care about me"
- Partner says "I need space," EM thinks "They need to self-regulate" not "They're abandoning me"
- Coworker declines to help, EM thinks "They have their own priorities" not "They're selfish"

**Recognizes boundary challenges often come from those who benefited from previous lack of boundaries**
- EM sets new boundary with parent, parent pushes back, EM recognizes parent benefited from old dynamic, doesn't give in
- EM stops over-functioning at work, boss complains, EM recognizes boss relied on their unpaid labor, holds firm
- EM starts saying no to friend who always asks for favors, friend gets angry, EM recognizes friendship was imbalanced, doesn't revert to old pattern

**Compassion for self and others during adjustment periods**
- EM's boundaries cause friction in relationships, EM recognizes this is normal transition, doesn't panic or apologize excessively
- EM gives others time to adjust to their new boundaries without rescuing them from the discomfort
- EM has compassion for those who struggle with boundaries (including self) without abandoning their own needs

## 49. Appropriate Privacy and Autonomy

**Maintains psychological distance while remaining connected**
- EM has close relationship but also private thoughts and inner world that isn't fully shared
- EM respects partner's right to have thoughts they don't share
- EM doesn't demand total transparency as proof of love

**Respects decision-making autonomy within relationships**
- EM and partner have different preferences for weekend plans, EM suggests but doesn't demand their preference
- EM supports partner's choice even when EM disagrees: "I think that's risky, and it's your decision"
- EM doesn't need to control partner's choices to feel secure

**Balances transparency with individual privacy needs**
- EM shares important information with partner (health, finances, feelings) but doesn't report every thought or interaction
- EM has friendships that don't include partner, doesn't hide them but doesn't merge them
- EM maintains personal journal or private space that is theirs alone

## 50. Boundary Violation & Repair Capacity

**Identifies boundary violations as red flags requiring attention**
- EM's partner reads their phone without permission, EM recognizes this as violation, addresses it immediately
- EM's boss demands work on vacation, EM identifies this as boundary crossing, negotiates or pushes back
- EM's friend shares EM's private information, EM recognizes breach of trust, has direct conversation

**Has established consequences for boundary crossings**
- EM tells friend "If you share my confidences again, I won't be able to share them with you in the future"
- EM tells partner "If you yell at me, I'll leave the conversation until we can talk calmly"
- EM tells coworker "If you interrupt me again, I'll stop contributing to this meeting"

**Can engage in repair conversations when boundaries are accidentally crossed**
- EM crosses someone's boundary, recognizes it, apologizes specifically: "I violated your privacy by reading that. I'm sorry. It won't happen again"
- EM's boundary is crossed, they address it directly but also seek to understand: "You did X, which crossed my boundary. Can you help me understand what was happening for you?"
- EM and partner both cross each other's boundaries in fight, EM initiates repair: "We both messed up. Can we talk about how to prevent this?"

## 51. Avoids Enmeshment

**Maintains clear boundaries with appropriate permeability**
- EM is close with family, has own home and life, visits regularly but doesn't live with them or rely on them financially
- EM has intimate relationship, maintains separate friendships and interests
- EM is involved in community, doesn't lose individual identity in group

**Warmth and intimacy without compromising autonomy**
- EM loves deeply, also makes independent decisions
- EM is emotionally available, also has emotional privacy
- EM shares life with partner, also has internal life that is theirs

**Individual identity maintained within close relationships**
- EM is "partner," "child," "employee," "friend" but primarily is "self"
- EM's relationships enhance their identity, don't define it
- EM would be recognizable as themselves even if all current relationships ended

**Support provided without over-dependence**
- EM helps friends, doesn't become their therapist or savior
- EM supports partner, doesn't manage their emotions or life for them
- EM is there for family, doesn't sacrifice own wellbeing to maintain peace
