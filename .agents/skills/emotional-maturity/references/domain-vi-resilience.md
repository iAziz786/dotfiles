# Domain VI: Resilience and Coping (Traits 36-44)

Detailed examples for resilience and adaptive coping.

## 36. Psychological Flexibility (ACT)

**Adapts responses to serve valued goals**
- EM wants to be a present parent (value), gets a major work opportunity that requires travel, adapts by negotiating remote work or setting clear boundaries around family time
- EM values health, gets injured, adapts by finding new forms of exercise that work with limitations rather than giving up entirely
- EM values connection, moves to new city, adapts by joining groups and initiating contact rather than waiting for friendships to happen

**Core processes: acceptance**
- EM experiences chronic pain, accepts its presence while still pursuing valued activities (doesn't wait for pain to go away to live)
- EM has social anxiety, accepts it will always be somewhat present, goes to party anyway because connection matters
- EM failed at something important, accepts the failure and the feelings it brings, doesn't fight reality

**Cognitive defusion**
- EM has thought "I'm a failure," recognizes it as a thought, not truth—"I'm having the thought that I'm a failure"
- EM notices anxiety saying "Something terrible will happen," defuses: "That's anxiety talking, not prophecy"
- EM experiences self-criticism, observes it: "There's my inner critic again," doesn't argue with it or believe it

## 37. Growth Mindset

**Views challenges as opportunities for development**
- EM loses job, thinks "This is a chance to pivot to something more aligned with my values"
- EM gets rejected from program, thinks "I can improve my application and try again, or explore other paths"
- EM faces health crisis, thinks "This is teaching me what's truly important"

**Belief in malleable vs. fixed abilities**
- EM struggles with public speaking, thinks "This is a skill I can develop with practice" not "I'm not a public speaker"
- EM finds math difficult, thinks "I haven't learned this yet" not "I'm bad at math"
- EM receives critical feedback, thinks "This is information about my current performance, not my fixed capability"

**Lower psychological distress and greater resilience**
- EM experiences setback, recovers faster than those with fixed mindset
- EM takes on challenging projects, not afraid of failure
- EM seeks feedback to improve, doesn't avoid it to protect ego

## 38. Post-Traumatic Growth Capacity

**Can find meaning and strength through adversity**
- EM survives assault, becomes advocate for survivors, finds purpose in helping others
- EM loses parent to illness, becomes healthcare professional, honors their memory through service
- EM goes through bankruptcy, develops financial wisdom, helps others avoid same mistakes

**Greater appreciation of life**
- EM survives serious illness, no longer takes health for granted, celebrates small daily joys
- EM experiences near-death experience, reprioritizes relationships over career
- EM faces loss, learns to love more openly, doesn't wait to express affection

**Improved relationships**
- EM's trauma reveals who their true friends are, deepens those bonds
- EM's vulnerability about their struggle invites others to be vulnerable, creates intimacy
- EM learns to ask for help, which strengthens relationships rather than weakens them

## 39. Adaptive Coping Flexibility

**Matches coping strategy to stressor controllability**
- Controllable problem (deadline at work): EM uses problem-solving, time management, breaks task into steps
- Uncontrollable problem (rain on outdoor event): EM uses acceptance, reframing ("cozy indoor party"), finding silver lining
- Mixed problem (partner's job loss): EM uses both problem-solving (updating resume together) and emotional support (listening, validating)

**Uses problem-solving, acceptance, cognitive reappraisal, social support**
- EM faces financial stress: makes budget (problem-solving), accepts some things can't be changed (acceptance), reframes "scarcity" as "simplification" (reappraisal), talks to financial advisor and partner (social support)
- EM experiences grief: allows feelings (acceptance), finds meaning in loss (reappraisal), joins support group (social support), handles practical matters (problem-solving)

**Problem-focused vs. emotion-focused coping based on appraisal**
- EM evaluates "Can I change this?" If yes—action. If no—acceptance and emotional processing
- EM doesn't try to control the uncontrollable (weather, other people, past)
- EM doesn't just accept when action is possible (doesn't become passive)

## 40. Tolerance for Ambiguity/Uncertainty

**Functions effectively without complete information**
- EM is offered a job without knowing all the details, can make decision based on available information and risk tolerance
- EM starts relationship without knowing if it will last forever, can commit to present without demanding future certainty
- EM invests money without knowing exact returns, accepts calculated risk

**Perceives ambiguous situations as desirable rather than threatening**
- EM is given vague project guidelines, sees it as creative freedom rather than anxiety-inducing lack of structure
- EM meets new people without knowing if they'll be friends, enjoys discovery process
- EM travels without rigid itinerary, enjoys spontaneity

**Shows adaptability, better decision-making in uncertain environments**
- EM's industry undergoes disruption, they adapt skills and pivot rather than clinging to obsolete role
- EM faces unexpected life change, adjusts plans fluidly
- EM makes decisions with incomplete data, updates beliefs as new information arrives (Bayesian thinking)

## 41. Constructive Failure Processing

**Extracts lessons without self-devaluation**
- EM fails exam: "I need to change my study strategy. This doesn't mean I'm stupid"
- EM's business fails: "I learned what doesn't work. Market conditions were tough. I'm not a failure as a person"
- EM gets rejected romantically: "We weren't a match. That doesn't mean I'm unlovable"

**Attributes failure to controllable, specific, temporary factors**
- "I didn't prepare enough" (controllable, specific) vs. "I'm not smart enough" (global, fixed)
- "The timing was wrong" (temporary) vs. "I always mess things up" (permanent)
- "My presentation skills need work" (specific) vs. "I'm terrible at everything" (global)

**Maintains self-efficacy beliefs after setbacks**
- EM fails, still believes they can succeed with adjustment
- EM experiences rejection, still believes they're worthy of love
- EM makes mistake, still believes they're competent overall

## 42. Realistic Optimism

**Maintains positive expectations while acknowledging reality**
- EM plans for success AND prepares for obstacles: "I believe I can get this job, and I'm also applying to other places just in case"
- EM starts business with confidence, also has emergency fund in case it takes longer than expected
- EM enters relationship with hope, doesn't ignore red flags or assume perfect outcome

**Belief in positive outcomes tempered by accurate risk assessment**
- EM is optimistic about treatment outcome AND informed about side effects
- EM expects good things AND plans for contingencies
- EM hopes for the best AND prepares for the worst (not pessimistic, just prepared)

**Flexible adjustment of expectations based on evidence**
- EM's project isn't working, they adjust timeline and resources based on new data rather than blind persistence or premature quitting
- EM's relationship has problems, they address them realistically rather than hoping they'll magically resolve or assuming it's doomed
- EM updates beliefs as evidence accumulates, doesn't cling to optimism in face of clear negative data

## 43. Self-Care Discipline

**Proactive maintenance of physical and emotional resources**
- EM schedules regular medical checkups before problems arise
- EM takes vacation days before burning out, not after
- EM maintains friendships regularly, not just when they need something

**Core domains: physical, emotional, social, professional**
- Physical: exercises regularly, sleeps 7-8 hours, eats nutritiously even when busy
- Emotional: has therapy or reflective practice, sets boundaries, processes feelings
- Social: nurtures relationships, has community, asks for help when needed
- Professional: continues learning, maintains competence, finds meaning in work

**Self-compassion mediates relationship between self-care and well-being**
- EM doesn't guilt themselves when they skip self-care, just restarts gently
- EM enjoys self-care without seeing it as indulgent or selfish
- EM recognizes self-care isn't selfish—it's necessary for sustainable giving

## 44. Somatic Intelligence

**Awareness and regulation of bodily stress signals**
- EM notices tightness in chest, recognizes it as anxiety, takes action before it escalates
- EM feels fatigue, honors it and rests rather than pushing through with caffeine
- EM notices clenched jaw, consciously relaxes it, realizes they're stressed

**Accurate interoception (awareness of internal bodily signals)**
- EM can distinguish hunger from boredom, thirst from fatigue, anxiety from intuition
- EM notices heart rate increasing during conversation, realizes they're triggered, takes break
- EM feels "butterflies" in stomach, recognizes it as excitement or fear, can name which

**Body-based emotional regulation**
- EM uses breathing techniques to calm nervous system
- EM goes for walk when agitated, knows movement helps process stress
- EM practices progressive muscle relaxation before sleep
